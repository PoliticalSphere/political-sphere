## üéØ Purpose

This Business Model Overview documents the intended revenue approach and the guiding principles that will fund and sustain Political Sphere. It explains the single-tier subscription model we intend to use, why that model is chosen, and the constraints we place on monetisation so the simulation remains fair, and accessible. The primary audience for this document includes the developer, contributors, community members, and potential partners who need a clear, auditable statement of the project's funding strategy and ethical boundaries.

Key commitments:

- Equal access: a single subscription grants full access to the game ‚Äî no paid advantages or feature gating.
- Independence: no advertising, sponsorships, political donations, or other revenue streams that could influence gameplay or governance.
- Transparency: pricing, cost allocation and monetisation decisions are communicated openly to players and stakeholders.
- Sustainability: revenue should reliably cover operating costs and enable responsible, long-term development.
- Ethical design: monetisation will never be tied to influence, progression, or political outcomes.

The chosen approach is intentionally simple and conservative: modest, affordable subscription revenue funds stewardship of the simulation rather than short-term monetisation. A known challenge is that many similar multiplayer political simulations rely on voluntary donations or complex monetisation strategies; later sections outline mitigations and community-focused tactics to address that risk.

### Business Model

Political Sphere operates on a single-tier subscription model.

One subscription gives full access to the game and all core features.

No micro-transactions, DLC, advertising, sponsorships, political donations, or external revenue streams.

No monetisation tied to influence, progression, or political outcomes.

The financial structure is intentionally simple, ethical, and transparent.

## üíº Operational Model

### Current Value Proposition

**A persistent multiplayer political simulation game grounded in UK parliamentary procedures, offering fair, skill-based gameplay with AI assistance.**

### Target Customers

Individuals interested in political simulation, strategy, governance, and structured role-based decision-making.

- **Community-driven players**: Value systems, realism, reputation, and long-form progression
- **Strategy enthusiasts**: Seek competitive and cooperative political dynamics

### Revenue

Primary & only revenue source: modest monthly subscription fee paid by players.

### Pricing Principles

- **Affordable and inclusive**: Stable and predictable pricing
- **Not dependent on exploitation**: No spending tiers or paid advantages
- **Reflects sustainability**: Covers running costs + fair margin
- **Prioritizes accessibility**: Over aggressive monetisation

### Player Experience Impact

All players have equal access to gameplay systems.

No paid advantage, paid authority, or premium power features.

Simulation remains merit-based, skill-based, and fair.

### Ethical Commitment

This model supports:

- Fairness
- Anti-manipulation
- Respect for democratic simulation integrity
- Player trust
- Transparency

No financial gatekeeping of power or voice.

Players pay for access‚Äînot influence.

### Long-Term Sustainability

The goal is not maximising revenue per user, but:

- Stable growth
- Healthy recurring income
- Long-term platform maintenance
- High trust and low churn

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How does the subscription model ensure fairness?

**A:** The single-tier subscription provides equal access to all features for all players. There are no premium tiers, paid advantages, or monetization tied to gameplay outcomes. This ensures merit-based competition and prevents financial barriers to participation.

### Q: What happens if players can't afford the subscription?

**A:** The pricing is designed to be affordable and inclusive. We focus on broad accessibility while maintaining sustainable operations. The model prioritizes quality over aggressive monetization.

### Q: How do you maintain independence from external influences?

**A:** No advertising, sponsorships, political donations, or external revenue streams. The business model is intentionally simple and transparent, with revenue coming solely from player subscriptions.

### Q: What if the game becomes very popular?

**A:** Growth will be sustainable and quality-focused. We prioritize player satisfaction, ethical standards, and simulation integrity over rapid expansion. The single-tier model ensures fairness regardless of scale.

### Q: How can players provide feedback on pricing or business decisions?

**A:** Players can share feedback through community channels. Business decisions are made transparently with consideration for player input, though the core ethical model remains non-negotiable.
