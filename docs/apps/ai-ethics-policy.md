# AI Ethics Policy

## Overview

This document outlines the ethical guidelines and principles governing the use of AI systems within the Political Sphere platform. Our commitment is to ensure AI systems are developed and deployed responsibly, transparently, and in alignment with human values.

## Core Ethical Principles

### 1. Transparency and Explainability

- All AI decisions must be explainable to human operators
- Users must be informed when interacting with AI systems
- Decision-making processes must be auditable and traceable
- AI-generated content must be clearly labeled

### 2. Fairness and Bias Prevention

- Regular bias audits of AI models and training data
- Diverse and representative training datasets
- Mitigation strategies for identified biases
- Equal treatment regardless of protected characteristics

### 3. Privacy and Data Protection

- Minimal data collection for AI training and operation
- Secure data handling with encryption and access controls
- User consent for data usage in AI systems
- Right to erasure and data portability

### 4. Safety and Harm Prevention

- AI systems must not cause physical or psychological harm
- Content filters to prevent harmful or abusive outputs
- Fallback mechanisms for AI failures
- Human oversight for critical decisions

### 5. Accountability and Governance

- Clear responsibility for AI system development and deployment
- Regular ethical reviews and audits
- Incident response procedures for AI-related issues
- Stakeholder involvement in AI governance

### 6. Neutrality and Censorship Resilience

- AI systems must remain neutral in political discourse
- Protection against censorship and manipulation
- Balanced representation of diverse viewpoints
- Resistance to external influence attempts

## Implementation Guidelines

### AI Development Process

1. **Ethical Review**: All AI features require ethical review before development
2. **Bias Assessment**: Training data and models assessed for bias
3. **Transparency Requirements**: Explainability features built into systems
4. **Testing**: Comprehensive testing including adversarial inputs

### Monitoring and Auditing

- Continuous monitoring of AI system performance
- Regular audits for bias and ethical compliance
- User feedback mechanisms for ethical concerns
- Incident logging and analysis

### Human Oversight

- Human-in-the-loop for critical AI decisions
- Appeal mechanisms for AI-generated outcomes
- Expert review panels for complex cases
- Training for users and operators

## Compliance Evidence

### EU AI Act Compliance

- **High-risk AI systems**: Identified and documented
- **Transparency obligations**: Met through explainability features
- **Data governance**: Compliant with GDPR requirements
- **Risk management**: Comprehensive risk assessments conducted

### Audit Trail

- **System inventory**: Complete catalog of AI systems
- **Decision logs**: Traceable AI decision-making processes
- **Training records**: Documentation of model training and validation
- **Incident reports**: Detailed records of AI-related incidents

## Enforcement

### Violations

- **Minor violations**: Warning and corrective action required
- **Major violations**: System shutdown and investigation
- **Critical violations**: Legal action and regulatory reporting

### Reporting

- **Internal reporting**: Anonymous channels for ethical concerns
- **External reporting**: Public disclosure for significant issues
- **Regulatory reporting**: Compliance with legal reporting requirements

## Review and Updates

This policy is reviewed annually or when significant changes occur in:

- AI technology landscape
- Regulatory requirements
- Organizational practices
- Stakeholder feedback
