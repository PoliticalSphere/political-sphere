## Strategic Overview

Political Sphere‚Äôs pricing philosophy is built on one non-negotiable idea:

Every player gets full, equal access to the complete experience ‚Äî no paywalls, no power boosts, no monetised influence.

There are no cosmetics, no ads, no loot boxes, and no premium gameplay advantages.
The game is financed solely through a single, modest monthly subscription.

This keeps incentives pure: development prioritises integrity and depth, not monetisation tricks.

## Core Pricing Model

| Aspect              | Description                                                               |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Model               | Subscription-only (B2C individual players)                                |
| Tiering             | One flat tier; all players receive identical features                     |
| Trial               | Optional short free trial (e.g. 7‚Äì14 days) with full access               |
| Price Philosophy    | "Modest, predictable, ethical" ‚Äî affordable for students and young adults |
| Billing Frequency   | Monthly (annual optional later)                                           |
| Cancellation Policy | Cancel anytime, no penalty, access continues until end of period          |
| Refunds             | Simple, transparent, documented in fairness policy                        |
| Tax Handling        | Inclusive pricing; regional adjustments later if required                 |

**Example phrasing (for docs):** ‚ÄúOne fair price for everyone. No tiers. No tricks. No power for sale.‚Äù

## Packaging Strategy

There are no content tiers or feature packages.
Every subscriber receives full access to:

- All core gameplay systems (debates, votes, parties, elections)
- Historical world archives
- Reputation and role progression
- Moderation and appeals tools (when in relevant roles)
- Party management and leadership systems
- Optional single-player sandbox (future)

**Why this matters:** The simulation relies on equality. Creating tiers breaks legitimacy.

## Fairness Principles Behind Pricing

- **Equality of Access:** Every player, regardless of means, experiences the full world.
- **No Financial Influence:** Money cannot buy credibility, power, or outcomes.
- **Predictability:** No hidden fees, upsells, or token systems.
- **Transparency:** Pricing visible and explained publicly.
- **Ethical Use of Funds:** Subscription revenue funds moderation, security, and development ‚Äî not manipulation or advertising.

## Operational Considerations (Zero-Budget Stage)

### Before Launch

- No payment processor until open beta.
- Early closed tests: free, invite-only.

### After Launch

- Track only: active subscribers, churn rate, free-trial conversion, MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue).
- Infrastructure: No premium server segregation. No additional fees for private worlds. If private hosting emerges, still within the single-tier subscription framework (player-to-player world hosting, not monetised).

## Accessibility & Inclusivity in Pricing

- Keep price low enough for students and early-career adults.
- Allow temporary grace periods for lapsed payments (7‚Äì14 days).
- Explore community-subsidised access later (not donations, but opt-in sharing schemes).
- Regional pricing parity possible in later phases if infrastructure supports it ‚Äî but never cheaper = less content.

## Communication Strategy

Position the pricing as a trust pact with players:

‚ÄúYou pay a small subscription to keep the world fair, safe, and running ‚Äî not to buy advantage.‚Äù

Language should highlight: fairness, simplicity, integrity, freedom from manipulation.

Avoid: marketing spin, fear-based messaging (‚Äúsupport us or we shut down‚Äù), elitism (‚Äúpremium access‚Äù).

Tone: calm, principled, transparent.

## Prohibited Monetisation Models

These are explicitly off-limits and should appear in every governance doc:

- Ads
- Microtransactions
- Cosmetic DLC
- Institutional licences
- Data monetisation
- Political sponsorship
- Pay-to-skip or boost
- NFT / blockchain

## Long-Term Extensions

If revenue scales ethically, future optional add-ons (still flat-access) may include:

- ‚ÄúFamily / Household Plan‚Äù (shared sub for multiple accounts)
- ‚ÄúSupporter Tier‚Äù (same content, optional higher payment for those who wish to fund development)
- ‚ÄúLifetime Membership‚Äù (if recurring billing becomes complex)

But these must never affect gameplay, moderation influence, or political power.

## Governance of Pricing

Pricing changes must follow a transparent internal process, such as:

- Documented rationale in governance register
- 30-day public notice before change
- No retroactive effect
- Maintained affordability principle
- Recorded as an ADR (Architectural Decision Record)

This keeps financial decisions ethically auditable ‚Äî same as code and rules.
