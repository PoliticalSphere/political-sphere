This document establishes guidelines and templates for external communication about AI governance in the Political Sphere project, ensuring transparency, accountability, and compliance with ISO 42001:2023 AI Management System requirements.

## Overview

Effective external communication about AI governance builds trust, demonstrates accountability, and meets regulatory transparency requirements. This framework provides structured approaches for communicating AI governance practices, decisions, and performance to external stakeholders.

## Communication Objectives

- **Build Trust:** Demonstrate commitment to ethical AI practices
- **Ensure Transparency:** Provide clear information about AI systems and governance
- **Meet Compliance:** Satisfy regulatory disclosure requirements
- **Manage Expectations:** Set realistic expectations about AI capabilities and limitations
- **Facilitate Dialogue:** Enable constructive stakeholder engagement

## Communication Framework

### Core Principles

#### 1. Transparency

**Guidelines:**

- Provide clear, accurate information
- Avoid technical jargon or explain it
- Disclose limitations and uncertainties
- Be honest about capabilities and risks

#### 2. Accessibility

**Guidelines:**

- Use plain language and clear explanations
- Provide information in multiple formats
- Ensure content is culturally appropriate
- Make information available in multiple languages

#### 3. Timeliness

**Guidelines:**

- Communicate proactively, not reactively
- Provide regular updates on significant developments
- Respond promptly to inquiries and concerns
- Meet regulatory reporting deadlines

#### 4. Accountability

**Guidelines:**

- Take responsibility for AI system performance
- Provide mechanisms for redress and appeals
- Demonstrate continuous improvement
- Maintain audit trails of communications

### Communication Types

#### 1. Proactive Communications

**AI Governance Reports**

- **Frequency:** Annual
- **Purpose:** Comprehensive overview of AI governance practices
- **Content:** Governance framework, performance metrics, improvement initiatives

**Transparency Disclosures**

- **Frequency:** Quarterly
- **Purpose:** Regular updates on AI system performance and governance
- **Content:** Key metrics, incidents, improvements, stakeholder engagement

**Educational Content**

- **Frequency:** Ongoing
- **Purpose:** Build understanding and awareness of AI practices
- **Content:** Explanations, case studies, best practices, user guides

#### 2. Reactive Communications

**Incident Communications**

- **Trigger:** AI system incidents or issues
- **Purpose:** Inform affected parties and demonstrate response
- **Content:** Incident description, impact assessment, response actions, prevention measures

**Regulatory Responses**

- **Trigger:** Regulatory inquiries or requirements
- **Purpose:** Provide required information and demonstrate compliance
- **Content:** Requested data, compliance evidence, corrective actions

**Public Inquiries**

- **Trigger:** Stakeholder questions or concerns
- **Purpose:** Address specific issues and provide clarification
- **Content:** Direct responses, additional context, follow-up actions

## Crisis Communication Protocol

### Incident Classification and Response Times

| Severity      | Response Time | Communication Channels     |
| ------------- | ------------- | -------------------------- |
| P0 (Critical) | 15 minutes    | All channels, immediate    |
| P1 (High)     | 1 hour        | Primary channels, urgent   |
| P2 (Medium)   | 4 hours       | Primary channels, standard |
| P3 (Low)      | 24 hours      | Standard channels, routine |

### Crisis Communication Team

#### Core Team

- **Communications Lead:** Overall coordination and messaging
- **Technical Lead:** Technical information and updates
- **Legal Counsel:** Regulatory compliance and legal considerations
- **Executive Sponsor:** Senior leadership representation

#### Extended Team

- **AI Ethics Officer:** Ethical implications and user impact
- **Security Lead:** Security-related incidents
- **Operations Lead:** System recovery and status updates
- **Customer Support:** User communication and support

### Communication Protocols

#### 1. Initial Response (Golden Hour)

**Objectives:**

- Acknowledge the incident
- Provide initial impact assessment
- Demonstrate active response
- Set expectations for updates

**Key Messages:**

- We are aware of the issue
- We are actively investigating
- We will provide regular updates
- We apologize for the inconvenience

#### 2. Investigation Phase

**Objectives:**

- Provide factual information as available
- Explain technical aspects clearly
- Maintain transparency about uncertainties
- Demonstrate thorough investigation

**Communication Guidelines:**

- Stick to confirmed facts
- Avoid speculation
- Use clear, non-technical language
- Provide regular updates even if no new information

#### 3. Resolution Phase

**Objectives:**

- Announce resolution and recovery
- Explain root cause and prevention measures
- Provide compensation or remediation where appropriate
- Restore confidence

**Post-Incident Activities:**

- Detailed incident report
- Lessons learned communication
- Process improvements announcement
- Follow-up with affected stakeholders
